Frequently Asked Questions

What does collision repair include?

Collision repair encompasses a range of services tailored to the damage sustained, including straightening bent metal components, replacing irreparable parts, repainting affected areas, and, if required, inspecting the chassis for structural integrity. The extent of the work varies based on the collision’s severity and impact forces.

How long does collision repair typically take?

Minor repairs are generally completed within 1–3 working days. More significant damage, such as that requiring part orders or intricate procedures, may take a week or more, depending on the complexity and scope of the repairs.

Will insurance cover collision repairs?

Most comprehensive or liability insurance policies cover collision repair expenses. We advise reviewing your policy’s terms and deductible. Our team is available to assist with insurance-related inquiries—please contact us promptly after any incident.

Is inspection required after collision repair?

A follow-up inspection at a certified station may be necessary if repairs involve structural or safety-critical components. This is not mandatory unless explicitly required by your insurance provider.

Can a car be repaired without insurance?

Yes, repairs can proceed without insurance, with all costs directly payable by the customer. We provide a detailed estimate beforehand to help you evaluate the repair’s value.

Do you provide a warranty for the work?

We offer a one-year warranty on all repair work, excluding rust repairs, which carry a separate warranty tailored to their specific nature.
